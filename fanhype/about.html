About fanhype

This was a project created for Dr. James Caverlee's Fall 2013 Information Retrieval course. The goal of the project was to solve a problem using non-trivial data that implemented one of the algorithms that was discussed in class.

We decided to implement an application that observed the amount of activity, or hype, that a certain fanbase was generating during a football game. We gathered tweets for both teams during football games, and sorted them by who they were hyped for.

We also created an algorithm that observed tweets and gave them a hype ranking based on the tweeter's popularity and the quality of their tweet.

The project was created by Brian Bell, William Guerra, and Jason Harris.
